joshua tree kombucha

As the UI/UX Designer for Joshua Tree Kombucha (JTK), I led a design team to improve the digital interface and experience of the company’s website.

JTK prides itself as a resource hub and its small-batch kombucha brewed with natural, organic ingredients—all handcrafted with integrity.

The Challenge

The interface and flow of the website is not intuitive for users.

The current website is outdated and does not interact well with users. The navigation is difficult and does not communicate a strong and memorable experience for users.

Challenge 1. Call-to-action

The call-to-action powers the landing page. It sets off a trigger for visitors to do something when visiting the page. The current landing page does not have a CTA.

Challenge 2. Photo Gallery

Currently, there is no photo gallery to display any of the products or community Usability test participants indicated that they would prefer to have more photos of the products and the kombucha community.

Challenge 3. User Flow

When users purchase products, they are redirected to a different site. This makes it more inconvenient and difficult for users to purchase products quickly and efficiently.

Project Goal

Provide a more intentional design and intuitive experience for users.

The website will introduce a new information architecture, resolve core challenges, and improve the visual design to ease the user experience.

Design Flow

Research

Intensive research was performed to understand the users' pain points, behaviors, and needs.

Through methodical and investigative approaches, we gained more insight to ensure how design decisions can further benefit the user.

Primary Research

In order to find major issues of the current design, I ran a moderated usability test with 12 participants using Lookback. The purpose was to understand and identify potential pain points of target users.

Analysis

With analytical information, I was able to create designs on a foundation of real, proven data.

Analytics provide concrete details to understanding the efficacy of a design and its relation to the user.

Design

I created the information architecture, wireframes, and designs of the the major screens.

I used Figma for the UI/UX design. These helped form the basis of the site's design.

Information Architecture

Based on the feedback and analysis from users, I created the information architecture with the level of importance in mind. The site is arranged in a way so that users naturally gravitate to the most important elements.

Low-Fid Wireframes

The low-fidelity wireframes sketch the general look of the major screens and are used as a guide for the overall site.

Mid-Fid Wireframes

The mid-fid wireframes architect the framework of the site. These wireframes are a cleaned up version of the low-fid sketches.

Core Features

A call-to-action, photo gallery, and improved user flow were implemented.

The main components of the site were improved using the features below:

1. Call-To-Action

I implemented a call-to-action (CTA) on the landing page to cue the visitors in on what they should do. When visitors click on the CTA, they are directed to the products page.

2. Photo Gallery

I integrated JTK’s Instagram to the website using a photo gallery, which was indicated to be important to the users. Visitors can also click on the photo and take a closer look at the photo.

3. User Flow

I designed a system for users to view products and purchase them without having to go to a different site. By doing this, it cuts the third-party merchants out, and the business can sell directly to consumers

Visual Guideline

Color palette and typography was carefully selected to best reflect JTK's branding.

The visual design plays an integral role in the correct reflection of the business. The color palette and typography, for example, should have the right balance in communicating to users the products and how to navigate the site.

Color Palette

The landing page in the before photo had a deep, dark background with a high contrast in white font. The color palette did not capture the freshness and simplicity that JTK intended.

The new color palette features a well-balanced combination of colors that are visually interesting with intentionality. When visitors think of kombucha, they should feel revitalized and happy. To demonstrate that intent, I chose a color palette that would convey exactly the mood and theme of the company.

Typography

The revised landing page has a visual hierarchy, readable fonts, and digestible information blocks that make the website scannable and eye-pleasing.

When users visit the landing page, they first see “BREW” and then their eyes are drawn to the caption: “everything DIY kombucha, FAQ Recipes.” Last, the next line users are drawn to is the CTA to trigger the visitor to view the products for purchase.

Reflection

Overall, the design changes helped boost sales and resulted in a 27% increase in profit margins.

If there’s anything I’ve learned from design, it’s that it’s so important to fall in love with the process, and not the idea.

Main Takeaways

1. Communication

Through this experience, I learned how important it is to be in constant and clear communication with your teammates. I’m very grateful that my team and I were constantly communicating and working together throughout each reiteration.

2. Dynamic Site

The new website became more dynamic with less steps required for users to purchase a product. The site was straightforward and intuitive for users. We supported those changes with usability testing to make sure there was data to back it up.
